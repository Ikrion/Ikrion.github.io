Inline Skate was first invented by a inventor named John Joseph Merlin in 1760 where he debuted it while attending a masquerade party. But the first actual patent issued in france for a roller skate was granted to a Monsieur Petitbled in 1819. In 1823, Robert John Tyers was first to receive an English roller-skate patent that have 5 wheels with the center wheel being larger than the rest to allow better maneuvering by shifting weight forward or backward.

Type of inline skating

Urban Skating AKA recreation skating

This type of skating is the most common and basic type of skating done. It is done mostly anywhere in the city like on the road, along the pavement or in a wide empty area. People can skate to work, together with friends or group, or just for fun.

Freestyle Skating

Freestyle skating is a form of inline skating where skater are performing tricks around a straght line of cones. The tricks are called slalom moves and the skates that are used for this have a tight fit boots with strong cuff to give more ankle support.

There are 4 different categories for this discipline in singapore.

  • Classic slalom
  • Speed slalom
  • Battle slalom
  • Freestyle Slide

Aggressive Inline Skating

Aggressive Inline Skating is similar to freestyle but instead of doing tricks with cone, you are doing tricks with jump and grind along objects. The skates that is used have an area in the frame between inner wheels that locks on obstacle and grinds this is called a "H-Block".

The tricks that can be done is also quite similar skateboarding tricks, which is can done in the street or in a skate parks.

Figure Skating

Inline figure skating is the same as what ice skating does it is just on different surface. You can do it alone, as a duo, or in a group.

The skates that is used is generally made of leather, there is a toe stop in front of the skate, and depending on what you are performing the boots can be stiff to support the ankle or softer to allow more ankle mobility

Skate component

The boots is where your foot is in contact with the skate. Depending on what type of skating you are doing the boots material will also be different. Generally the boots it is high and soft to provide ankle support and for greater comfort. There is also harder boots that help to protect the foot against impact or to help control the skate better

The Cuff is the upper part of a skate that help with ankle support

There is 2 fasteners. 1st is to tighten the cuff, the 2nd one is to tighten the boots together like tying shoelace

The shell is to provide support to your ankle and hold everything together

The frame is the support that your wheels is attached

This component is an optional attachment. It mainly just give you another way to stop if you are not confident in doing a brake maneuver

Wheel size vary depending on what you are using your skate for. Wheel size can go from 44mm up to 150mm.
